Properties of Matrix Multiplication

  1. If the product AB exists then it is not necessary that the product BA will also exist.
  2. Matrix multiplication is not commutative even if AB and BA exist, they need not be equal.
  3. Matrix multiplication is associative i.e., A(BC) = (AB)C.
  4. Let A be a square matrix then A2 = A.A , A2.A = A.A2 = A3.
  5. (Am)n  =  Amn  ; Am . An = Am+n
  6. A(B+C) = AB + AC [Distributive Law]
  7. A is a matrix of order m x n then A.In = Im.A = A. If A and I are of same order then AI = IA = A. I is called multiplicative identity.
